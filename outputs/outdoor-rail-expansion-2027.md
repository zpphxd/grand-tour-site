## 2. Swiss rail hub: facts, boundaries and maintenance

The rail hub should help a traveler choose the right **type of product**, then hand off to an official seller for a live quote. It must not present a cached fare as a guaranteed price or the open timetable feed as a booking confirmation.

### Decision model

| Traveler need | Explain first | Important caveat | Official source |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fixed journey | Point-to-point ticket versus Supersaver | Supersaver availability and discounts vary and the ticket is tied to a selected connection | [SBB Supersaver tickets](https://www.sbb.ch/en/tickets-offers/tickets/point-to-point-switzerland/supersaver-tickets.html) |
| Flexible day of travel | Saver Day Pass | Capacity-priced; date and Half Fare status affect the offer | [SBB Saver Day Pass](https://www.sbb.ch/en/tickets-offers/tickets/day-passes/saver-day-pass.html) |
| Frequent Swiss travel | Half Fare Travelcard or GA Travelcard | Compare total trip economics; these are not automatically the best visitor products | [SBB travelcards](https://www.sbb.ch/en/tickets-offers/travelcards.html) |
| Visitor resident abroad | Swiss Travel Pass | Eligibility, duration and included services must be checked live | [SBB Swiss Travel Pass](https://www.sbb.ch/en/tickets-offers/tickets/guests-abroad/swiss-travel-pass.html) |
| Cross-border trip | Through ticket versus separate tickets | A through ticket may offer better connection protection; sales windows and conditions vary by operator | [SBB tickets for Europe](https://www.sbb.ch/en/tickets-offers/tickets/europe.html) |
| Overnight trip | Night train and berth type | Reservations are compulsory on many night services and berth inventory is date-specific | [SBB night trains](https://www.sbb.ch/en/leisure-holidays/europe/night-trains.html) |
| Bicycle journey | Bike ticket plus any bicycle reservation | Passenger and bicycle tickets are separate concerns; route, train type and seasonal reservation rules vary | [SBB bicycle tickets](https://www.sbb.ch/en/tickets-offers/tickets/bikes/bike-ticket.html) |

### Data and UX rules

- Continue using [transport.opendata.ch](https://transport.opendata.ch/docs.html) for useful timetable discovery, but label it as an open/community interface, not an SBB booking or disruption guarantee.
- Where richer Swiss transport data is required, assess the licensing and feeds in the [Open Transport Data Switzerland platform](https://opentransportdata.swiss/en/) before adding a new provider.
- Every live result needs `provider`, `retrieved_at`, origin/destination IDs and a direct official booking handoff.
- Fare cards need `verified_on`, the assumptions used (date, class, passenger, reduction card) and “check live price.” Never hardcode a fare or discount percentage into evergreen prose.
- Separate the resident model from the visitor model. Ask country of residence, trip length, expected travel days, party composition, class and Half Fare status before comparing products.
- Show an itinerary confidence state: live result, cached result, schedule-only, or editorial example.
- Treat bicycle carriage as a first-class constraint. Ask whether the bike is assembled, bagged or rented locally and surface reservation requirements before checkout handoff.
- Night-train advice should prioritize berth inventory and compulsory reservations, not merely departure time.
- International split-ticket advice must explain the protection tradeoff; never recommend a cheaper split without exposing separate-contract risk.
- Add a source drawer on every advice card so changing SBB conditions can be audited without rewriting the interface.

### Rail revalidation cadence

- Product eligibility, validity and refund/exchange rules: monthly and at every release.
- Live fares, inventory and reservations: at user search time; cache only briefly.
- Timetables: refresh daily in the near term and annotate planned versus real-time information.
- Bicycle restrictions and seasonal reservation rules: monthly in cycling season and at checkout handoff.
- Night-train routes: monthly, then daily during timetable changes or disruption periods.
- Editorial examples: expire after 30 days unless explicitly marked historical.

## 4. Minimum destination schema

Every hub and route should support:

- stable hub ID, coordinates, nearest rail station and airport
- ride styles, season, weather window and crowd profile
- route distance, ascent/descent, surface, technical difficulty and fitness demand
- heat, wind, snow, altitude, exposure, traffic and closure risks
- official GPX/map source, emergency guidance and bailout points
- bike rental, transfer, storage and rail-carriage information
- stable hub facts separated from event date, registration window and live availability
- `source_url`, `source_type`, `verified_on`, `next_check_at`, `confidence` and editorial owner
- booking URL separated from the source used to verify a fact

Commercial rental operators can be booking options but should not be the authoritative source for traffic rules, closures, event dates or safety. Do not use Strava as a public route catalogue.
